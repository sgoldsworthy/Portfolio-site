Casino Early Life

Built an analytics framework for casino customer acquisition and early-life optimisation — acquisition channel analysis, retention modelling, LTV forecasting, and A/B testing to improve onboarding and marketing allocation.

SQL Python Snowflake Power BI A/B Testing Cohort Analysis

Business Problem

The casino business acquired thousands of new customers monthly but had limited understanding of what drove early-life churn and long-term value. Which acquisition channels produced quality players? How did individual game volatility affect behaviour? Where exactly were players dropping off — and could we intervene before they left?

Why It Was Difficult

  • Player behaviour varied enormously by acquisition channel, game type, and volatility exposure
  • The most critical churn window turned out to be the first hour — far more granular than standard cohort windows
  • Game-level volatility analysis required understanding of mathematical properties alongside behavioural data
  • Required constant stakeholder collaboration — this was not a self-service project but an iterative, decision-driven engagement

My Role

I owned the analytical framework end-to-end: acquisition channel analysis, player behaviour modelling, game volatility impact assessment, gap analysis, and retention/LTV measurement. This was a highly stakeholder-facing project — working iteratively with commercial and product leads to shape decisions rather than just deliver reports.

Approach

Player Behaviour Analysis

  • Built granular first-hour, first-session, and first-day behavioural tracking — identifying that the majority of churn occurred within the first 60 minutes
  • Extended to standard 7/14/30/90-day cohort windows with real-time updates
  • Mapped player journeys across game types, session patterns, and deposit behaviour

Game Volatility & Gap Analysis

  • Analysed individual game volatility and its effect on player behaviour and early-life dropout
  • Identified gaps in the game portfolio where player demand existed but product supply was weak
  • Quantified how high-volatility early experiences accelerated churn for certain player segments

Acquisition Channel & Retention

  • Decomposed LTV and retention by acquisition channel to identify which sources produced sustainable players
  • Built A/B testing frameworks to evaluate onboarding interventions
  • Created channel-level scorecards linking spend to long-term quality metrics

Product & Commercial Actions

Technologies

  • SQL — Complex cohort construction, window functions, real-time aggregations
  • Python — Statistical analysis, volatility modelling, A/B test evaluation
  • Snowflake — Data platform and computation
  • Power BI — Reporting and stakeholder-facing visualisations

Outcome

  • Identified first-hour churn as the primary dropout point — led to targeted onboarding interventions
  • Game volatility insights influenced portfolio decisions and default game recommendations for new players
  • Acquisition channel analysis redirected marketing spend toward higher-LTV sources
  • Gap analysis identified product opportunities that were subsequently prioritised by the roadmap team

Lessons Learned

  • The most impactful insights came from going more granular than anyone expected — first-hour analysis revealed patterns invisible at daily/weekly resolution
  • Game volatility is a player experience problem, not just a mathematical property — it directly drives early churn for inexperienced players
  • Stakeholder-driven projects deliver more impact than self-service tools when the decisions are complex and require commercial judgement
  • A/B testing in casino requires careful control for game mix and session depth, not just conversion rates
