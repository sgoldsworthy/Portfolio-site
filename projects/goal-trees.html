Strategy

Goal Trees

Designed a hierarchical KPI decomposition and data governance framework — defining, governing, and owning ~240 KPIs across the business, enabling executives to trace high-level outcomes to actionable operational levers.

SQL Snowflake Sigma Data Governance Semantic Layer Python

Business Problem

The executive team operated with high-level KPIs (revenue, margin, active customers) but had no structured way to decompose poor performance into root causes. When revenue dropped, the question "why?" led to weeks of ad-hoc investigation rather than a clear diagnostic path.

Why It Was Difficult

  • Business KPIs are interconnected — a drop in revenue could stem from acquisition, retention, pricing, product mix, or market conditions
  • No standard framework existed for hierarchical decomposition
  • Different teams owned different layers of the tree but didn't communicate effectively
  • Required both mathematical rigour (additive/multiplicative decomposition) and business pragmatism

My Role

I designed the goal tree framework, defined the mathematical relationships between metrics, built the data layer, and facilitated adoption across the leadership team.

Approach

Framework Design

  • Mapped high-level outcomes (revenue, margin) to their mathematical components
  • Defined each level of decomposition with clear ownership
  • Ensured every metric at every level had a governed definition

Mathematical Structure

  • Used multiplicative decomposition where metrics multiply (e.g., Revenue = Customers × ARPU)
  • Used additive decomposition where metrics sum (e.g., Revenue = Casino + Sports + Poker)
  • Built variance analysis that automatically identifies the largest contributors to change

Operationalisation

  • Built interactive Sigma dashboards that allow drill-through from top to bottom
  • Automated variance flagging to highlight where attention is needed
  • Integrated with forecasting to show expected vs. actual at each tree level

Architecture

CEO-Level KPIs (Revenue, Margin, Growth)
      ↓
Business Unit Decomposition (Casino, Sports, Poker)
      ↓
Product Metrics (ARPU, Retention, Conversion)
      ↓
Operational Levers (Campaigns, Features, Pricing)
      ↓
Team-Level Actions

Technologies

  • SQL — Metric calculations and hierarchical aggregations
  • Snowflake — Semantic layer and governed metric definitions
  • Sigma — Interactive drill-through dashboards
  • Python — Automated variance analysis and alerting

Outcome

  • Leadership able to diagnose performance issues in minutes rather than weeks
  • Clear accountability: every metric has an owner at every level
  • Reduced "metric confusion" — eliminated debates about which number is correct
  • Framework adopted as the standard for all business reviews

Lessons Learned

  • The framework only works if metric definitions are unambiguous at every level
  • Executive buy-in requires showing the tree in their language (commercial), not yours (technical)
  • Not every metric decomposes cleanly — be honest about where the framework approximates
  • Variance analysis is more valuable than the tree itself — executives care about "what changed" more than "what is"
