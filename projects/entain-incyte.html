Product

Entain Incyte

Designed and built a self-service analytics product combining a governed semantic layer, automated AI narratives, forecasting, and Sigma dashboards for commercial stakeholders.

Snowflake Sigma Python SQL Semantic Layer AI Narratives

Business Problem

Commercial teams were drowning in dashboards but starving for insight. Hundreds of reports existed, but stakeholders couldn't answer fundamental questions without requesting analyst support. There was no single source of truth, no governed metrics, and no automated interpretation of what the data meant.

Why It Was Difficult

  • Over 200 existing dashboards with conflicting definitions
  • Multiple data domains (acquisition, retention, product, finance) with different owners
  • Stakeholders wanted narratives, not charts — "tell me what happened and why"
  • Required real-time data freshness with governed, trustworthy metrics

My Role

I led the product vision, architecture design, metric governance, and delivery of Incyte. This included defining the semantic layer, designing the narrative generation system, building the forecasting engine, and managing stakeholder adoption.

Approach

Metric Governance

  • Audited all existing metrics across the business
  • Established single definitions with clear ownership
  • Built a governed semantic layer as the single source of truth

AI Narratives

  • Developed automated narrative generation for multiple cohort views: Cohort Overview, First 7/30/90 Day LTV, Lifetime LTV, Retention, Product-level narratives (Casino, Sports, Poker, Bingo), Multi-product cross-sell
  • Each narrative adapts based on early-life vs. mature cohort data patterns

Forecasting

  • Integrated statistical forecasting models
  • Automated anomaly detection with contextual explanations

Technologies

  • Snowflake — Data platform, semantic layer, dynamic tables
  • Sigma — Self-service BI and exploration
  • Python — Narrative generation, forecasting models
  • SQL — Complex cohort analytics and metric definitions

Outcome

  • 60% reduction in time from question to answer
  • 246 governed KPIs with clear ownership
  • Self-service adoption across commercial, product, and finance teams
  • AI narratives reduced reliance on analyst interpretation
  • Single source of truth eliminated conflicting numbers

Lessons Learned

  • Governance must come before self-service — without trusted metrics, self-service creates more confusion
  • Narratives are more powerful than charts for executive audiences
  • The hardest part isn't building the technology — it's getting agreement on metric definitions
  • Adoption requires ongoing stakeholder education, not just a good product
